Emulator Reviews

Whether you're just getting started with handheld emulators or you're a seasoned retro gamer, this section is your guide to the devices that bring classic games back to life. From compact powerhouses like the Miyoo Mini to versatile options like the Anbernic RG35XX, Here you'll find a break down of what each emulator does well, where it falls short, and who it's best for. These reviews are based on hands-on experience and trusted community insights, so you can find the right fit for your retro adventures!

Miyoo Mini Plus

A pocket-sized retro powerhouse with a crisp display and impressive battery life. Perfect for quic pick-up-and-play sessions. It runs everything from NES to PS1 with ease! Menu navigation can feel clunky without OnionOS, but once installed, it's a dream!

Miyoo Mini

The original fan-favorite that kicked off the Miyoo craze. Its small size makes it ultra-portable, and the D-pad is best-in-class. However, it's harder to find in stock and lacks Wi-Fi without mods.

Link to a video review or article Link to a purchase unit

Anbernic RG35XX

A Game Boy-inspired handheld that nails the nostalgia factor. Fantastic screen and solid build quality and a great starter device for newcomers. Just be ready for some tinkering to get GarlicOS or other custom firmware running well.

Link to a video review or article Link to a purchase unit

Retroid Pocket 5

A hybrid Android handheld that plays PSP, Dreamcast, and even some GameCube. Great for those who like flexibility and modern emulation with Play Store access. The interface can be overwhelming for beginners, and button feel is subjective.

Steam Deck

A beast of a device that's more gaming PC than retro handheld. Handles everything from emulation to AAA games with customizability to spare. It's large and not exactly pocketable â€” but for power users, it's unbeatable.
